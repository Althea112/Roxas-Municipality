Introduction

Roxas is a first-class municipality in the province of Isabela, Philippines. Known for its rich agricultural lands and warm, vibrant community, Roxas continues to grow as one of the most progressive towns in the region. It is especially famous for celebrating the annual Pinakbet Festival, showcasing its deep connection to farming and cultural heritage.

Geography and Economy

Strategically located in the northeastern part of Luzon, Roxas is blessed with fertile lands suitable for farming. The town is a leading producer of rice, corn, and vegetables in Isabela. Its local economy thrives on agriculture, trade, and small enterprises.

"The land of golden grains and cultural pride."

People and Culture

The people of Roxas are known for their hospitality and strong sense of community. The municipality takes pride in its Ilocano and Ibanag roots, blending traditional customs with modern aspirations. The annual Pinakbet Festival reflects this identity through music, food, and vibrant street performances.

Festivals - Roxas, Isabela

Festivals of Roxas, Isabela

Pinakbet Festival

The Pinakbet Festival is an annual celebration in Roxas, Isabela, showcasing the iconic Ilocano dish “Pinakbet.” It highlights the town’s agricultural roots and Ilocano traditions through colorful street dancing, parades, local delicacies, and community performances. The festival brings the community together in a vibrant and joyful cultural display.

Araw ng Roxas

Araw ng Roxas marks the founding anniversary of Roxas, Isabela, and is celebrated with civic activities, parades, contests, and night programs. Every year, different artists are invited to perform on stage, entertaining the community with live music and shows. The celebration is a symbol of unity and pride for every Roxaseño.
